# Malvin-Telecom-SQL-analysis
This project analyzes customer churn at Maven Telecom to understand why customers are leaving, who is most at risk, and the resulting revenue impact. The goal was not just to calculate churn metrics, but to build a reusable, production style analytics pipeline using SQL Server and Power BI.

### Introduction
Customer retention is critical for sustained growth in the telecom industry. Despite this, customer churn is inevitable and can significantly impact revenue and long term profitability.

### ðŸ“ˆ Key Insights

- Maven Telecom has **1,869 churned customers**, and **20% of them are high value customers**
- **42% of churned customers left within the first 6 months**
- The **top three churn reasons** are:
  - Competitors offered better deals
  - Competitors provided better devices
  - Poor attitude of support staff
- Maven lost approximately **$1.7 million in revenue to competitors**, making competitor driven churn the most expensive
- The strongest churn indicators are:
  - Month to month contracts
  - No premium tech support
  - Fiber optic internet users
  - No promotional offer or Offer E
- **70% of customers who churned to competitors were Fiber Optic users**
- High value customers are churning at a rate of **23%**
- Among **1,250 remaining high value customers**, **77% are classified as high risk** based on churn indicators

### ðŸ›  Customer Retention Strategies

**1. Loyalty Programs**  
Since competitors offering better deals is the top churn driver and many churned customers had no promotions, Maven should introduce loyalty programs. These may include discounts, free upgrades, or additional features for long term customers.

**2. Improve Customer Support**  
Invest in training and performance incentives for support staff to improve service quality and customer satisfaction.

**3. Better Devices**  
Evaluate and upgrade device offerings to remain competitive in terms of performance, features, and pricing.

**4. Expand Premium Tech Support**  
Customers without premium tech support churn more frequently. Offering this service more broadly could significantly reduce churn.

**5. Improve Fiber Optic Services**  
Enhance fiber optic reliability, speed, and customer support to reduce churn in this high risk segment.

**6. Engage High Value Customers**  
Provide personalized offers, proactive communication, and premium support to retain high value customers.

**7. Strengthen After sales Engagement**  
Implement regular customer check-ins via surveys, calls, or emails to monitor satisfaction and resolve issues early.
